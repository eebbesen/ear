The Five Secrets of Effective Communication

("EAR")

E = Empathy
  1. The Disarming Technique: Find some truth in what the other person is saying, even if it seems totally unreasonable or unfair.
  2. Empathy: Put yourself in the other person's shoes and try to see the world through their eyes.
    • Thought Empathy: Paraphrase the other person's words
    • Feeling Empathy: Acknowledge how the other person is probably feeling, based on what they said.
  3. Inquiry: Ask gentle, probing questions to learn more about what the other person is thinking and hearing.
A = Assertiveness
  1. "I feel", "I think", "I need" statements: Express your own ideas and feelings in a direct, tactful manner. User "I feel" statements, such as "I feel upset," rather than "you" statements, such as "You're wrong!" or "You're making me furious!"
R = Respect
  1. Respect: Convey and attitude of respect, even if you feel frustrated or angry with the other person. Find something genuinely positive to say to the other person, even in the heat of battle.


Bad Communication* Good Communication
  1. You fail to express your feelings openly and directly using "I Feel" Statements.
  1. You express your feelings in an open, direct and respectful way using "I Feel" Statements.
  1. You ignore the other person's feelings. Instead you argue defensively for your "truth".
  1. You acknowledge the other person's feelings and find the truth in what they said.
  1. Your attitude is not respectful or caring. Instead you convey hostility, competitiveness, or sarcasm.
  1. Your attitude is respectful and caring, even if you feel angry or frustrated.


The Bad Communication Checklist*
Communication Error Communication Error
  1. Truth: You insist you're "right" and the other person's "wrong".
  2. Blame: You claim the problem is the other person's fault.
  3. Defensiveness: You argue and refuse to admit any imperfection.
  4. Martyrdom: You claim you're an innocent victim.
  5. Put-Down: You imply the other person is a loser.
  6. Labeling: You label the other person as a jerk or loser.
  7. Sarcasm: Your tone of voice is belittling or patronizing.
  8. Counterattack: You respond to criticism with criticism.
  9. Scapegoating: You imply that the other person is defective or has "a problem".
  10. Diversion: You change the subject or list past grievances.
  1. Self-Blame: You act as if you're awful and terrible.
  2. Hopelessness: You claim you've tried everything and nothing works.
  3. Demandingness: You complain that people aren't the way you expect them to be and insist they should be different.
  4. Denial: You say you don't feel angry, sad or upset when you do.
  5. Helping: Instead of listening, you give advice or "help".
  6. Problem-Solving: You try to solve a problem and ignore feelings.
  7. Mind-Reading: You expect your partner to know what you want and how you feel.
  8. Passive-Aggression: You say nothing, pout or slam doors.
